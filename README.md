## Research Experience

### Current Research (Ph.D. Period)

**1. Bayesian Search for IMBHs in LIGO data**  
*Published in MNRAS* | [arXiv:2107.12109](https://arxiv.org/abs/2107.12109)

*Implemented and ran a Bayesian search for IMBH in O2 data.*
- Ran ~0.2M Bayesian PE analyses, totaling 1.2M core-hours
- In addition to finding previously detected GW events, we discovered a few new GW candidates
- Code: [git.ligo.org/avi.vajpeyi/bcr_pipeline](https://git.ligo.org/avi.vajpeyi/bcr_pipeline)

**2. Measuring properties of AGN disks with gravitational waves**  
*Published in ApJ* | [arXiv:2111.03992](https://arxiv.org/abs/2111.03992)

*Constructed a stellar mass BH population model for BH merging in AGN disks.*
- Simulated two AGN black hole populations
- Demonstrated O(200) events required before AGN parameters can be measured
- Code: [github.com/avivajpeyi/agn_phenomenological_model](https://github.com/avivajpeyi/agn_phenomenological_model)

**3. Deep Follow-up for Gravitational-wave Inference: A Case Study with GW151226**  
*Published in ApJ* | [doi:10.3847/1538-4357/acbdf4](https://doi.org/10.3847/1538-4357/acbdf4)

*Devising and utilising a "deep-followup" Bayesian analysis method on GW candidates.*
- Demonstrate the mathematical path to constrain a few dependent priors
- Analysing GW events with ambiguous PE results using GW151226 as a case study
- Code: [github.com/avivajpeyi/deep_gw_pe_followup](https://github.com/avivajpeyi/deep_gw_pe_followup)

**4. TESS-Atlas**  
*In prep*

*Obtaining Bayesian PE fits and cataloging all TESS Objects of interest with a transit model.*
- First Bayesian PE catalog for all TESS objects of interest
- Fitting for systems with data for multiple-transits, single-transits, and multiple-planets
- Code: [github.com/dfm/tess-atlas](https://github.com/dfm/tess-atlas)
